Mock Job Drive

A non-technical event aimed at pre-final year students for getting a taste of the current campus hiring process.

Event type: Non Technical
Participation: Individual, Any department
Platform: TBD
No. of Rounds: 4
Date/Time: Jan 22nd, 9am to 12pm


Round 1
Aptitude test(also contains technical MCQ) in Microsoft form- validation to be done automatically in the form itself.[ around 200 participants expected in this round itself], only the top 40 percent qualifies for the next round

Round 3
Technical Interview round, a fair and unbiased interview would be conducted for this student by experienced final year students who are placed in super dream companies for software development roles to select the top 10 candidates for the final HR round. This round would be conducted in zoom and a panel of 2-3 members would judge them i.e for each panel member a definite number of candidates would be allocated. 


Round 2
Group discussion for about 30 minutes, the participants would be divided into groups of 8 and would be moderated by volunteers present in each such group, only 25 percent of people from each group would qualify for the next round. This event would be conducted as different zoom calls for each group with a moderator, i.e 8-9 different zoom calls for group discussion.

Round 4
HR round, a fair and unbiased interview would be conducted for this student by experienced final year students who are placed in Management Companies to select the top 3candidates. This round would be conducted in zoom and a panel of 2 members would judge them i.e for each panel member a definite number of candidates would be allocated.
Note: Candidates would be judged on various metrics whichwould be preset like subject knowledge, communication skills,etc. which would be fair and impartial.

Hackers' Asylum

Hackers' Asylum

Event type: Technical
Participation: Individual, Any department
Platform: TBD
No. of Rounds: 1
Date/Time: Jan 22nd, 9am to 1pm


Event Description:
This is a CTF(Capture the flag) style Cyber Security event. Theme of the event is that a medical research lab had successfully found a cure for cancer. But just before the formula was published, a doctor on the research team stole the formula and disappeared after clearing out all files on the research computer. The lab has hired the participant to investigate the computer and recover the formula.The participants have to uncover clues, keys and other “flags” to uncover the puzzles one byone. Each puzzle is a security puzzle which includes OSINT, password cracking, Brute Forcing etc.
Participants will receive points for each puzzle they crack. The participants do not need anyprior knowledge except basic computer and browsing skills. They can ask for help anytime but points will be calculated accordingly. The aim of this event is to create an interest in the cyber security domain.

(1 hour qualifying round + 3 hours Final Hacking round)

Paper Presentation

A paper presentation event is a technical event that provides the participants with a platform to showcase their research ideas in front of an esteemed panel of judges/experienced academicians/researchers.

Event type: Technical
Participation: Teams of 1-3, Any department
Platform: Zoom
No. of Rounds: 2
Date/Time: Jan 23rd, 9am to 1pm



Shortlisting Criteria/Paper Evaluation:
The papers received from the participants will be evaluated by the coordinators to check if they are in accordance with the specified format of the paper(IEEE standards). The papers will subsequently be checked for plagiarism by means of plagiarism detectors available online. The papers which are not plagiarized and in accordance with the specified format will then be evaluated based on innovation scope and feasibility. The final shortlist count will be based on the time allocated for the event.


Description:

The interested participants will be asked to submit their papers via email(to the mail id provided) The received papers would then be split accordingly among event coordinators to validate them against the shortlisting criteria specified above. The shortlisted participants will then be contacted via email by the event head to confirm their selection. They will then be asked to prepare a PPT presentation for their research topic and submit the same two or three days prior to the event to the event head via email. (Non-submission of PPT in time will also lead to elimination) The shortlisted participants will then be shared Zoom links to the event. During the Zoom call, they would be presenting the prepared PPT before the audience(other shortlisted participants) and the panel of judges(yet to be decided) in turns and will be on call till the event concludes. The decision of the panel will remain final and accordingly the winner will be declared.
